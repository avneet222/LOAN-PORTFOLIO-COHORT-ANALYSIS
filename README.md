# 1. Executive Summary
This report presents a comprehensive cohort analysis of the loan portfolio for the period March to May 2025, covering 11,643 loan applications. It covers five key areas: monthly trends, bureau score performance, geography, insurance type, and BRE decisions, providing data-driven insights and recommendations.

Key Findings : In this context, Bureau Score and BRE decision — is the single strongest predictor of loan performance. The 500-649 (Fair) score band is completely blocked (0% settlement), while the Red BRE category shows an 86% rejection rate, indicating well-calibrated risk controls. The primary opportunity lies in improving discharge approval rates and addressing the sharp May-25 settlement drop.
# 2. Monthly Cohort Analysis
2.1 Loan Volume Trend
The portfolio grew significantly from March (5,048 loans) to April (5,783 loans), representing a 14.6% month-on-month increase. May 2025 shows only 812 loans, which is expected as the month may not be complete at the time of data extraction. April represents the strongest month in the portfolio period.

# 3. Bureau Score Band Analysis
3.1 Settlement Rate by Score Band
Bureau score bands reveal the most critical finding in this report: the 500–649 (Fair) score band has a 0% settlement rate and 100% rejection rate across 705 applications. This indicates a hard policy block on this segment. All other bands show reasonable settlement rates ranging from 38.6% (No Score) to 57.5% (Poor, <500), with Very Good (750–799) showing the highest settlement rate at 56.6%.

3.2 Discharge Amount by Score Band
Higher bureau scores correlate with higher average discharge amounts, which follows intuitive logic — financially responsible borrowers tend to have larger and more complex claims. The 800+ (Excellent) band shows the highest average discharge of ₹32,487, which is 53% higher than the No Score segment at ₹21,214.

CRITICAL INSIGHTS : The 500-649 Fair score band (705 loans, 6.1% of total) is completely blocked with 100% rejection. While this may be intentional policy, it represents a significant volume of applicants receiving zero service. The Poor (<500) band paradoxically shows a higher settlement rate (57.5%) than the Good (650-749) band (46.4%), suggesting score bands alone may not be optimal risk predictors in this portfolio.

Recommendations 

✓	Review the 500-649 block policy — if intentional, ensure applicants are clearly communicated the rejection reason and offered alternative products or improvement pathways.
✓	Investigate why the Poor (<500) band outperforms the Good (650-749) band in settlement rate — this is a counter-intuitive finding that may reveal a gap in the scoring model.
✓	Prioritise the 750-799 and 800+ bands for premium processing tracks — they show the highest discharge amounts and lowest rejection rates.
✓	For No Score applicants (97.5% admission approval), develop a separate underwriting track with additional manual checks given the absence of bureau data.
# 4. Geographic Analysis — Top 10 States
4.1 Volume and Settlement by State
Kerala dominates the portfolio with 6,990 loans (61.5% of total), followed by Karnataka (2,575) and Tamil Nadu (387). Telangana shows a concerning pattern: it has the lowest settlement rate (26.0%) among all states, the highest average final bill (₹1,80,434), and a high rejection rate (18.7%). Andhra Pradesh also shows a below-average settlement rate of 30.8% despite a moderate discharge amount of ₹51,928.

# 5. Insurance Type Analysis
5.1 Corporate vs Retail
Both Corporate and Retail insurance types show 100% admission approval rates, which is a distinctive characteristic of this segment — all insured applicants are admitted. The key differentiation appears at the discharge stage. Corporate insurance achieves a 87.1% discharge approval rate versus 84.6% for Retail, and correspondingly, Corporate shows a higher settlement rate of 81.3% vs 80.4% for Retail. Critically, both types show 0% rejection rates — a stark contrast to the overall portfolio rejection rate of 13.4%, indicating that insurance-backed loans are processed through a completely separate, higher-approval pathway.

# 6. BRE Decision Analysis
6.1 Green, Amber, and Red Cohorts
The Business Rules Engine (BRE) classifies applications into three decision categories. Green (auto-approve) accounts for 53.9% of volume with a 57.4% settlement rate and 0% rejection. Amber (manual review) accounts for 32.4% with a 51.7% settlement rate and 5.3% rejection. Red (decline) accounts for 13.6% of volume but shows an 86.0% rejection rate and only 8.6% settlement — confirming the BRE is performing its risk-filtering role effectively.

INSIGHTS : The Red BRE category still shows 14.0% admission approval — meaning 222 out of 1,584 Red-flagged applications were admitted. Of these, only 8.6% ultimately settled. This suggests either the BRE Red classification is being overridden manually in some cases, or there is a gap in the admission gatekeeping logic that needs addressing.

Recommendations

✓	Investigate the 14% admission approval within the Red BRE category — identify who is overriding BRE decisions and under what authority. If override is policy, document and track separately.
✓	For Amber cases, reduce manual review TAT (currently 1,473 minutes LBA TAT vs 1,013 for Green) — faster decisions will improve discharge completion rates.
✓	Use Green BRE settlement benchmarks (57.4%) as the target for Amber process improvement — the goal should be bringing Amber closer to Green outcomes through faster, cleaner processing.
✓	Validate BRE rule thresholds quarterly — the fact that Red cases have a higher avg final bill (₹96,089) than Amber (₹84,786) may suggest high-value edge cases are being incorrectly classified as Red.
